# Digital Chandru - Interview (Behavioral)

## 1. Handling Deadlines
I approach engineering deadlines the same way I approach my fitness routine at the gym: consistency and measurement over heroic last-minute sprints. I break down large architectural tasks into testable, deployable chunks. If a deadline is extremely tight, I don't sacrifice code quality or skip error handling; instead, I aggressively scope down features. I will ship a rock-solid, limited-feature V1 rather than a buggy, full-feature V1. 

## 2. Technical Disagreements
I try to remove emotion and ego from technical disagreements by relying on data and profiling. For example, if there's a debate over whether to use a Vector DB or keyword search for a specific feature, I don't argue philosophy. I will spin up a quick script, run both against 1,000 edge-case queries, profile the latency and accuracy, and let the numbers dictate the architecture.

## 3. Continuous Learning
The AI space moves blindingly fast. I don't chase every new tool on HackerNews. I read core research papers on weekends to understand the fundamental math and architecture changes (like new attention mechanisms). I only learn a new framework if it solves a specific, painful production bottleneck I am currently facing.
